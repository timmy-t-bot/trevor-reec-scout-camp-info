Trevor Reec Scout Camp Packing List - Week 2

Note: This comprehensive packing list is designed for Boy Scouts attending a camp at Trevor Reec during week 2. Since specific information about Trevor Reec camp could not be found, this list includes general essentials for summer Boy Scout camping with additional considerations for week 2 specific needs.

Clothing

Base Layers & Undergarments

  • 5-7 pairs of underwear
  • 5-7 pairs of socks (consider wool or synthetic moisture-wicking)
  • 2-3 pairs of camp pants/shorts
  • 3-5 shirts (T-shirts or moisture-wicking shirts)
  • 1-2 pairs of pajamas
  • 1 set of long underwear (in case of cool evenings)

Outerwear

  • 1 pair of swim trunks
  • 1 hat with brim (for sun protection)
  • 1 warm hat (for cool evenings)
  • 1 light jacket or sweatshirt (for evenings)
  • 1 rain jacket or poncho

Footwear

  • 1 pair of closed-toe hiking shoes or boots
  • 1 pair of camp/canvas shoes (like sneakers)
  • 1 pair of sandals (for showers and around camp)
  • Extra shoe laces (in case of breakage)

Personal Items

Hygiene & Grooming

  • Toothbrush and toothpaste
  • Soap or body wash (biodegradable)
  • Shampoo (biodegradable)
  • Deodorant
  • Comb or brush
  • Nail clippers
  • Sunscreen (very important!)
  • Insect repellent
  • Towels (1 bath towel, 1 face towel)
  • Wash cloth
  • Toilet paper (pack in ziplock bag)

Health & Safety

  • Personal medications (with instructions)
  • First aid supplies (bandages, antiseptic wipes)
  • Small flashlight or headlamp (and extra batteries)
  • Prescription glasses (if needed) and case
  • Contact lenses and solution (if applicable)
  • Small mirror

Camping Gear & Equipment

Sleeping Gear

  • Sleeping bag (rated for expected temperatures)
  • Sleeping bag liner (for added warmth and hygiene)
  • Sleeping pad or air mattress
  • Pillow or pillowcase to stuff with clothes

Camp Tools & Utilities

  • Water bottle (at least 1 liter capacity)
  • Canteen or hydration system
  • Eating utensils (spork or knife/fork/spoon set)
  • Plate/bowl (plastic or metal)
  • Cup/mug (plastic or metal)
  • Mess kit or aluminum foil squares for easy cleanup

Additional Gear

  • Small backpack or day pack
  • Rope (if allowed by camp rules)
  • Small knife or multi-tool (check camp policies)
  • Watch or clock
  • Sunglasses

Optional Items

Comfort & Recreation

  • Book or e-reader (limited use during camp activities)
  • Small journal and pen
  • Playing cards (camp-appropriate)
  • Small musical instrument (if permitted)
  • Camera (check camp policies on electronics)

Week 2 Specific Considerations

Special Items That Might Be Needed by Week 2

  • Extra durable clothing (items may be getting worn by week 2)
  • Additional blister treatment supplies (feet may be more tender)
  • Extra sunscreen (supply may be running low)
  • Extra insect repellent (bottle may be empty)
  • Additional ziplock bags for wet/dirty items
  • Small repair kit for gear (tape, needle and thread)
  • Hand sanitizer or wet wipes (for quick cleanups)
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • Extra toilet paper
  • Small stash of snacks for trading with other scouts (if permitted)

Important Notes

Prohibited Items

  • Electronics (phones, tablets, gaming devices) unless specifically permitted
  • Valuable jewelry
  • Knives or sharp objects (unless approved camping tools)
  • Candy or food not approved by camp staff
  • Aerosol cans
  • Matches or lighters
  • Cigarettes, alcohol, or drugs

Labeling

  • Label ALL items with your name using permanent marker
  • This helps with identification and prevents loss

Packing Tips

  • Pack in a duffle bag or backpack (check camp requirements)
  • Keep the packing list in your bag for reference
  • Bring items in waterproof bags or containers
  • Use compression sacks for sleeping bags and clothing
  • Coordinate with your patrol (if applicable) on shared items

Weather Considerations

Since Trevor Reec information is unavailable, prepare for typical summer camp conditions:

  • Hot days (sun protection is essential)
  • Cooler nights (warm layer needed)
  • Possibility of rain (waterproof gear essential)

Final Checklist Before Departure

  • All items labeled with name
  • Permission slips completed
  • Medical forms completed
  • Emergency contact information included
  • Camp fees and payment information packed
  • Transportation arrangements confirmed
  • Copy of the packing list included in bag

Conclusion

This packing list should cover all necessary items for a successful week 2 at Trevor Reec Scout Camp. Remember to check with your troop leaders or camp staff about any specific requirements or prohibited items at Trevor Reec. If you're unsure about any item, it's better to ask ahead of time rather than bring something inappropriate for the camp.
